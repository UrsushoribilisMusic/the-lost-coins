Chapter 14 - The Birthday Boy
========================================

The dining room at the Bürgenstock was an expanse of floor-to-ceiling glass, suspended over the abyss of the lake. Below, the lights of Lucerne twinkled like fallen stars, but inside, the world was amber-hued and heavy with the scent of expensive truffles and vintage Krug.

Lena had ordered the best of everything. She wasn’t just spending Zheng’s money; she was buying an alibi for her soul.

“To the key-hunter,” Lena toasted, raising her flute. “The man who finds what’s lost.”

Paul beamed, his face flushed with the kind of relief only a massive bank balance can provide. He knew the transfer was scheduled for the morning—a digital sunrise that would change his life. “I couldn’t have done it without the client’s faith,” he said, glancing at Lena, “and Erica’s patience.”

As the plates were cleared, they migrated to the lounge. The champagne was replaced by a parade of Islay malts, smoky and deep. The bartender, sensing the high-octave energy of the trio, kept the pours generous.

By the time they retreated to the Royal Suite, the world had slowed to a luxurious, intoxicating blur. The air in the suite was cool, but the atmosphere was electric.

“It’s officially the day,” Erica whispered, glancing at the clock as it ticked past midnight. She moved to the sideboard, her movements fluid and cat-like despite the whiskey, and pulled the cork on a final bottle of Bordeaux. “Happy birthday, Paul.”

They sang the song—a hushed, intimate version that felt more like a pact than a celebration.

Paul sat on the edge of the oversized bed, looking between the two women. The whiskey had stripped away his usual tech-guy awkwardness, leaving something raw and expectant. “So,” he murmured, his voice thick. “Do I get a present, or was the nine-course meal it?”

Erica started to offer a playful excuse about a forgotten gift, but Lena stepped forward. The “funk” that had been simmering since the lab, since the cafe, since the first handshake, finally boiled over.

“Actually,” Lena intervened, her voice steady but her heart drumming against her ribs, “we decided you deserved something... unique. A surprise.”

Erica’s eyes met Lena’s over the rim of her glass. There was a moment of silent communication—a shared spark of curiosity and mutual attraction. Erica wasn’t just going along with it; she was leaning in, her gaze lingering on Lena with an intensity that promised she was just as interested in the woman as she was in the man.

“A surprise?” Paul asked, his breath hitching.

“Off with the clothes, birthday boy,” Erica commanded softly, a mischievous glint in her eyes. “Orders from the board of directors.”

Paul didn’t hesitate. The room fell into a charged silence, broken only by the rustle of fabric and the distant hum of the lake breeze against the glass.

Lena felt the heat of the moment—the dangerous, beautiful entanglement Zheng had ordered, now fused with a desire she no longer wanted to fight. As they joined him, the boundaries between agent, target, and lover dissolved into the shadows of the suite.

In the soft glow of the bedside lamp, Lena leaned down, her lips brushing Paul’s ear while her hand found Erica’s.

“Happy birthday, Paul,” she whispered, as the world outside the room ceased to exist.
