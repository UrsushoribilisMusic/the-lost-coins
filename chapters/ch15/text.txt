Chapter 15 - The Alcor Infiltration
========================================

In Macau, the sun was setting, casting long, needle-like shadows across Mr. Zheng's obsidian desk. He didn't wait for the encrypted line to stabilize before he spoke.

"The asset is secure," Zheng said. "The scientist is already working with the dead. She has the tools to pry the pages of the mind apart."

On the screen, Mr. Gonzales appeared, silhouetted against a different horizon. He wasn't in the mountains; he was in a sterile, white-tiled corridor, speaking softly into a Bluetooth headset.

"Good," Gonzales said. "Because my side of the deal is already in motion. We've moved past simple access, Zheng. I'm not going to have your girl working in a high-security facility in Arizona with the FBI five minutes away. We're taking the prize with us."

Zheng leaned back, his eyes narrowing. "You're stealing a Dewar canister? Those things are the size of a small car. They weigh tons."

"We don't need the whole tank," Gonzales whispered, glancing over his shoulder. "My two guys inside—the security detail—they've mapped the rotation. Tomorrow night, during the scheduled nitrogen refill, we initiate the swap. We have a 'decoy' ready. A John Doe, similar build, same age of preservation. We'll switch the ID tags and move the real Finney into a portable cryogenic transport unit."

"And then?"

"Then he goes for a drive," Gonzales smiled, a flash of white teeth in the dark. "I have a property in the Sequoia woods. Deep in the California high country, miles from the nearest paved road. No sensors, no federal response times, no witnesses. Just your people, my security, and the frozen man."

Zheng tapped a rhythmic beat on his desk. "The Sequoia National Forest. It's remote. But it's a logistical nightmare to move lab equipment out there."

"That's why you pay for the best, amigo," Gonzales snapped. "I'm prepping a mobile lab in a refurbished old mill deep in the Sequoia forest. It's isolated, structurally sound, and off the grid. Your girl will have everything she needs. But you need to get them moving. Erica needs to start planning the 'research trip' now. It needs to look like a legitimate academic excursion to California."

Zheng stared at the digital ledger on his screen, the long-dormant Finnley address glowing like a radioactive sun. For twenty years, he had been the plumbing for the world's filth—moving money, scrubbing blood from bank accounts, and taking his percentage. He was tired of being the middleman.

He didn't just want a seat at the table; he wanted to own the room. The billions sitting in Finnley's untouched Bitcoin wallet represented more than wealth—they represented an exit from the underworld and an entry into a stratosphere of power where laws were merely suggestions. He was done laundering the fortunes of others. This was his "hit," the singular score that would turn a shadow-financier into a king.

Zheng felt a flicker of something resembling pity for Erica and Paul, quickly extinguished by the math of the "Whale" wallet. He picked up his phone and sent a short, encrypted message to Lena.

[11:42 PM] Zheng: The test is over. Tell Erica she's going to California. She needs to prepare for a deep-tissue scan on a high-priority donor. It needs to look official. Get the plane tickets.
