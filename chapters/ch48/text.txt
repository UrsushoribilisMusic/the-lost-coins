Chapter 48: The Napa Connection

The sterile white walls of the Mariposa County hospital felt like another kind of prison, but at least the air didn't smell like cordite. Lena sat on the edge of the exam table, her skin pale against the blue paper gown. The graze on her cheek from the shootout had been cleaned and bandaged, but the doctor was lingering over her charts with a puzzled expression.

"The scratch is fine," the doctor said, looking up from his tablet. "But we ran a standard blood panel before the localized anesthetic. Your HCG levels are elevated, Lena. You're pregnant. Very early—maybe three weeks."

The room seemed to tilt. Lena's hand instinctively went to her stomach. Three weeks. The math was instantaneous. It wasn't the long, desperate nights of the project's early stages. It was the night in Napa. The night they had all tried to forget the pressure of the "Whale" by losing themselves in each other.

"Are you sure?" she whispered.

"The labs are definitive," he replied. "I'll leave you a moment."

---

An hour later, the door creaked open. Paul, moving slowly with a heavy wrap around his torso, and Erica walked in. They looked exhausted, but there was a light in their eyes that hadn't been there before.

"Hey," Paul said softly. "Pinto told us you were still in the wing. We wanted to see how the cheek was doing."

Lena looked at them, her eyes red-rimmed. She looked at Paul—the man she had manipulated, protected, and ultimately grown to care for—and then at Erica.

"The cheek is fine," Lena said, her voice trembling. "But the doctor just gave me the rest of the results. I'm pregnant."

"Paul..." Lena said, her voice barely a whisper. "Three weeks. There hasn't been anyone else. It's from that night. It's yours."

The silence in the room was heavy. Paul's hand tightened on his crutch. He did the math in a heartbeat. Napa. The vineyard. The high-end suite where the lines between all three of them had dissolved for one complicated, beautiful night.

Erica didn't scream. She didn't lunge. She stood perfectly still, her hand still resting in the crook of Paul's elbow. She looked at Lena, then at Paul, and let out a long, slow breath. The anger from the dungeon had been replaced by a weary, cosmic irony.

"Well," Erica said, her voice surprisingly steady, though it had a sharp, dry edge. "I guess that's one hell of a birthday present for you, isn't it, Paul? A two-for-one special from Napa."

Paul looked at the floor, his face burning with a mix of shock and terror. Lena stuttered "Erica, I—"

"Stop," Erica interrupted, her eyes softening just a fraction. She looked at Lena. "If we're going to survive what comes next, we do it with the truth. Because there's something you should know, too." She squeezed Lena's arm. "I'm pregnant as well. I told the paramedics this morning."

Lena's jaw dropped. She looked from Erica's hand to Paul's stunned face.

"Two of them?" Chen's voice drifted in from the hallway as he leaned against the doorframe, having overheard the exchange. He shook his head, a look of pure, bewildered amusement on his face. "Paul, my friend... I told you the world needed more Pandas, but I didn't think you were planning on starting a whole sanctuary. You really don't do anything halfway, do you?"

The tension in the room didn't break, but it shifted. The three of them—the scientist, the spy, and the engineer—were now bonded by something much more permanent than a Bitcoin wallet or a Mafia heist.

"What happens now?" Lena asked, looking at the two people who were now her only real family.

"Now," Paul said, looking between the two women who now carried his future, "we survive the FBI depositions. And then... I guess we figure out how to be a 'we.' However messy that looks."
