Chapter 42: The Cluster

The morning sun fought through the thick canopy of the Sequoias, casting long, sharp shadows across the clearing of the old mill. At 09:30, the forest was deceptively still, but the air was humming with the hidden frequency of high-stakes violence.

From a ridge two hundred yards to the south, Agent Smith lowered his thermal binoculars. He was tucked into a blind of pine boughs, his tactical headset chattering with the status reports of his strike teams.

"Team One in position at the perimeter," a voice crackled. "Team Two is dark at the rear egress. We have eyes on the target building. No movement yet."

"Hold your positions," Smith commanded, his voice a flat, mechanical rasp. "We wait for the principals. I want Zheng and Gonzales in the same room before we breach. I need the visual on them together—I need the conspiracy charge to stick. Do not move until I give the word."

Simultaneously, at the trailhead three miles to the north, Sheriff Miller adjusted his duty belt. He stood next to his lead deputy and the Alcor Security Chief, who was checking the seal on a portable containment unit in the back of a white service van.

"Warrant is in hand," Miller said, his tone professional and weary. "We move in quiet, we serve the papers, we recover the property. If these contractors give us any lip, we zip-tie 'em and let the lawyers sort it out. Let's ride."

---

At 09:45, the silence of the mill was shattered by the gravel-crunching weight of two black SUVs. Gonzales stepped out of the lead vehicle, followed by his two nervous transporters. He didn't look like a man arriving for a meeting; he looked like a man arriving for a funeral. He marched toward the mill doors, his hand resting conspicuously on the butt of the sidearm holstered at his hip. Behind him, four of his soldiers fanned out, their eyes scanning the treeline with professional paranoia.

Five minutes later, a silver Mercedes S-Class glided into the clearing, flanked by two identical black E-Class sedans. It was a display of sheer, arrogant power. The doors opened in perfect synchronization. From the escort cars, six men in charcoal suits stepped out—Zheng's personal security detail—forming a tight, moving diamond around the silver car.

Zheng stepped out of the Mercedes, looking immaculate in his dark coat. He didn't wait for a greeting; he walked straight toward the entrance, his jaw set in a line of lethal greed.

"Target Z is entering the structure," Smith whispered into his comms. "Gonzales is already inside. This is it. All units, stay dark. Stand by for my command. I want them to start the meeting—I want them on the record before we blow the doors."
