Chapter 30 - The Principal's Arrival

The secure line crackles with the static of a trans-Pacific bounce. In the damp, shadowed interior of an abandoned logging warehouse deep within the Sequoia National Forest, Gonzales leans against a rusted forklift. Outside, the towering trees swallow the light. The living quarters smell of cedar and old dust.

"The delay is a problem, my friend," Gonzales growls into the encrypted handset. "My men are sitting around in this rot. The Apex unit is prepped and staged in the annex, the decoy is ready, and Alcor is quiet. But we can't move the pioneer without the synaptic mapping array. Every hour we wait is an hour for the humidity or the feds to catch us out here."

Thousands of miles away, in a penthouse overlooking the South China Sea, Zheng stares at a digital map of the Sequoia region. His face is a mask of cold, focused intent.

"The equipment was flagged in Oakland," Zheng's voice is precise. "A bureaucratic hiccup with the specialized cooling processors. My intermediaries are smoothing it over, but I do not trust smoothing from this distance anymore. Not for this project."

"What are you saying?" Gonzales asks.

"I am saying that I will no longer manage this by proxy. I am boarding my Gulfstream in an hour. I am coming to California to coordinate the delivery of the equipment myself. I have too much capital invested in Dr. Voss's hands to leave it to logistics firms."

Gonzales lets out a low chuckle. "You're coming to the woods? I'll have a bottle of tequila waiting, compadre. And a chair that doesn't have termites."

"I am not coming alone," Zheng adds, his tone chillingly flat. "I am bringing a crew. Loyal men who have been with me since Macau. If there is a bottleneck in the port or a leak in our security, they will handle it with the necessary precision."

"Understood. I'll clear the annex for your people."

"Seven days," Zheng confirms. "Let Lena keep the talent distracted. The Napa interlude was a necessity, but the honeymoon ends the moment I touch down. When I arrive, the clock starts."

Zheng ends the call. He looks out at the Macau skyline, then turns to the three silent men standing by his office door—his personal guard. He doesn't care about Erica's dreams of family or Paul's debts. To him, they are the finest tools money can buy. And he is finally coming to pick them up.
