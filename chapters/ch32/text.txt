Chapter 32 - The Room in the Clouds

The Fairmont is a temple of old San Francisco opulence—marble floors and hushed voices. Lena checks in with the seamless grace of a woman who belongs in five-star hotels. She's exhausted; the emotional intensity of the night before has left her craving a shower and a few hours of silence.

As she enters her suite on the fourth floor, the heavy door clicking shut, her phone vibrates. It's the encrypted line.

"Lena," the voice says. It's Zheng.

"I wasn't expecting a call until the equipment cleared," Lena says, dropping her keys on the mahogany desk and walking toward the window overlooking the city.

"The equipment is being handled," Zheng replies, his voice cold and efficient. "I am currently over the Pacific. I will be touching down in California in six hours to coordinate the delivery myself. I want the talent at the site first thing in the morning. Erica and Paul need to begin the calibration and mapping protocols immediately. Seven days, Lena. No more delays."

"I'll have them ready," Lena says. "We'll head to the Sequoias at first light."

The line goes dead. Lena stares at her reflection in the glass. The honeymoon hadn't just ended; it had been incinerated.

She turns toward the bedroom, reaching for the light switch.

"Don't bother with the lights, Ms. Vance," a voice speaks from the corner of the room.

Lena spins around. A man sits in the armchair—unassuming, tired suit, holding a badge that catches the dim light.

"Inspector Pinto," she breathes.

"And Special Agent Vance, FBI," a second man says, stepping out from the bathroom, blocking her exit.

"We know about the money, Lena. We know about Macau. We know about the cartels," Pinto says, standing. He stops just inches away. "We have enough to ensure you never see a sunset again. But we aren't here for you. We're here for the man you work with. The one who just called."

In the silence of the suite, the reality of her situation settles over her with the weight of a physical shroud. She knows the ledger: the shell companies in Cyprus, the wire transfers from the Sinaloa cartels, the talent she is currently trafficking across state lines.

She thinks about the ultrasound photo of her sister's baby. She thinks about the man in the air right now, bringing killers to a logging warehouse in the middle of nowhere.

A strange, dizzying sense of relief washes over her. The relief of the exhausted runner who finally sees the finish line.

"He's coming," Lena whispers.

Pinto's eyes sharpen like a hawk's. "Who is coming?"

"The man you're looking for," Lena says, her voice shaking but steady. "He's in the air. He'll be in California by midnight. If you want him... you're going to have to protect me. Because the moment I give him to you, I have nowhere else to go."
