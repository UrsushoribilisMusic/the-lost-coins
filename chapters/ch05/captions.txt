Past midnight. Paul hunches over glowing monitors.
Blue hex-code washes across his tired face.

The door creaks. Erica appears in silk.
Steam rises from her tea into darkness.

"Any luck?" she asks, voice heavy with sleep.
Paul's fingers dance across the mechanical keyboard.

"They sharded the keys. Broke them into pieces."
He explains: digital secrets split apart for safety.

"One shard is useless. Two might be enough."
His eyes reflect the scrolling encrypted data.

"Lagrange Interpolation. I can solve it mathematically."
Three blocks of code glow on his screen.

"If the threshold was two, I've won already."
The gamble hangs electric in the air.

Erica's hand touches his shoulder. A gentle kiss.
"Don't chase ghosts. I'll be in bed waiting."

Her nightgown flutters as she drifts away.
Paul doesn't look back. Already lost in code.

He's caught the scent. The hunt is real.
A key-hunter closing in on cornered prey.

The monitors pulse. His fingers fly faster now.
Deep in the architecture. Almost there. Almost.

The cursor blinks. One final command to execute.
Everything depends on what happens next tonight.
