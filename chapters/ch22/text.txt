Chapter 22 - Building Trust
========================================

They met at a secluded corner table in a dimly lit bar near the HSLU campus. The air smelled of expensive gin and rain. When Erica arrived, she looked tired but sharp, her professional armor firmly in place.

Lena didn't wait. She slid a thick, cream-colored folder across the table. Inside are the "Panama Dossiers"—impeccable forgeries of IRB approvals, a notarized "Last Will and Testament" of an anonymous tech pioneer, and a series of medical transport manifests stamped with federal seals.

Erica goes through them, her eyes scanning the legalese. "This looks... surprisingly thorough," she admits, her voice losing some of its edge. "But I still don't understand. Why us? There are research labs in the States—Stanford, MIT—that would kill for this kind of funding. Why bring a boutique team from Switzerland to a forest in California?"

Lena leans in, the candlelight catching the gold of her necklace. She lets her voice drop into a warm, intimate register.

"I asked around, Erica. Not just in the tech circles, but in the private investor community—the people who actually know who is moving the needle. I asked who the top synaptic researchers in the world are. Your name came up three times. They told me that while the big labs have the hardware, you have the intuition. They said you're the only one who treats the brain like a story instead of a circuit board."

Erica's gaze flickers. The professional compliment hit its mark, but it's the way Lena said it—with a focused, almost hungry intensity—that makes her breath hitch.

"And there's more," Lena continues, sensing the opening. "The family of the deceased... they aren't just looking for the money. They've pledged to donate a significant portion of the recovered funds to a new foundation. A foundation for neuro-regeneration research. They want you to head the European branch, Erica. This isn't just a job; it's a legacy."

It is a beautiful lie, draped in the promise of altruism.

"A foundation," Erica repeats, the idea of unlimited research freedom finally starting to erode her caution. She looks at Lena, the "funk" from the spa returning with a vengeance. "You're very good at this, Lena. The pitch, the paperwork... the flattery."

"It isn't flattery if it's true," Lena says softly. She reaches across the table, her fingers grazing Erica's wrist. She feels the pulse there—quick and thrumming.

Lena is "into" Erica; the mission has long since blurred into a genuine, terrifying attraction. She wants to protect her, even as she is hand-delivering her to Zheng.

Erica doesn't pull away. Instead, she turns her hand over, interlacing her fingers with Lena's. She is flattered—not just by the professional recognition, but by the fact that this sophisticated, dangerous woman seems so focused on her.

"I want to believe you," Erica whispers, her eyes searching Lena's. "And I want to see where this... where this leads."

"Then come to California," Lena says, her heart aching with the weight of the betrayal. "Trust the science. Trust me."

Erica looks at the folder one last time, then closes it. "I will think about it."
