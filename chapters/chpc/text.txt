Post-Credits: The Long Sleep
The Alcor Life Extension Foundation, Scottsdale, Arizona
Three Months After the Sequoia Incident

The hum of the facility was a constant, low-frequency vibration—the sound of a billion dollars worth of liquid nitrogen and redundant power systems keeping the future on ice. Here, there were no sirens, no shouting, and no "Big Fire."

The Alcor Security Chief walked down the sterile Corridor 4. He stopped in front of a massive, stainless steel dewar—Unit 001-H. Unlike the battered, bullet-riddled scrap metal back in California, this canister was pristine. There were no yellowed plastic labels here; only a laser-etched serial number and a secure digital interface that pulsed with a soft, steady green light.

"Status check," the Chief murmured.

The technician beside him tapped a tablet. "Internal temperature is stable at -196°C. Vitrification remains at 100% integrity. The specimen is undisturbed."

The Chief looked at the glass viewport, though he knew there was nothing to see but the swirling white mist of the cryogen. Inside lay the man who had changed the world with a few lines of code and then stepped out of time before the chaos could claim him.

"How's the legal team doing?" the Chief asked.

"Our lawyers are finishing the deposition for the federal prosecutors today," the technician replied. "They've built an airtight case against Zheng and Gonzales for the attempted theft of a 'High-Value Asset.' They're using the decoy swap as the primary evidence of intent. Since Zheng's people actually opened the decoy unit and tried to initiate a scan, our legal team has them on record attempting to 'plunder' the dead."

The Chief let out a short, dry snort. "Good. If they want to see what was in that van in California to prove their case, they can go to the FBI evidence locker and look at the remains of 'Big Maria.' Zheng wanted a legend; he got a mob bookkeeper."

The Chief tapped the side of the dewar—a respectful, quiet knock.

"Sleep tight, Hal," he whispered. "The world isn't ready for you to wake up yet. They're still fighting over the coins you left behind."

He turned and walked away, his footsteps echoing in the silence of the vault. Behind him, the green light on Unit 001-H continued to pulse—a slow, digital heartbeat in the dark, waiting for a future that was finally, slowly, beginning to learn its lesson.

THE END.
