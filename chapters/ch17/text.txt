Chapter 17 - The Cold Chain
========================================

The “Golden Hour” in Zug was cold and sharp. Lena stood on a footbridge overlooking the Sihl river, the wind whipping her coat around her legs. Her phone vibrated—the specific, low-frequency hum reserved for Zheng.

“The test is over, Lena,” Zheng said, his voice cutting through the wind like a blade. “It’s time for the California run. Get the hacker and the girl on a plane. I want them in the Sequoia high country by the end of the month.”

Lena’s grip on the railing tightened. “California? Zheng, that’s a logistical nightmare. Erica’s equipment—the synaptic mappers, the cryo-stabilizers—it’s not exactly ‘carry-on’ luggage. Moving that tech through US customs without a dozen federal red flags is impossible. We’ll be flagged before we leave the tarmac.”

“I don’t pay you to tell me what’s impossible,” Zheng replied, his tone devoid of patience. “We aren’t moving her lab. We’re building her a new one. Tell Erica she has a private benefactor—an anonymous donor who wants to fund a field study on deep-tissue synaptic mapping. Tell her to make a list. Every sensor, every electrode array, every liquid nitrogen centrifuge she needs.”

“A list?”

“A complete inventory,” Zheng confirmed. “I will have the hardware sourced through our channels in Shenzhen and shipped directly to a staging site in California. It will be waiting for her in a mobile unit. All she needs to bring is her proprietary software and her own expertise. To the university, it looks like a prestigious, fully-funded research sabbatical.”

“She’s smart, Zheng. She’s going to ask why a donor is being so... specific.”

“Then give her the right incentive. And Lena?” There was a pause, the kind that preceded a life-altering revelation. “Don’t let Paul get distracted by the market dips. Tell him the scale of this wallet.”

“How big are we talking?”

“Paul’s little recovery was a grain of sand,” Zheng whispered. “The Finney wallet... it’s an ‘O.G.’ stash from the genesis era. We aren’t looking for ten coins. We are looking for overone thousand.”

Lena felt the world tilt. 1,000 Bitcoin. At current market rates, even with the recent dip, that was nearly$60 million. A ten-percent commission would be enough to buy a small island. Her own five-percent finder’s fee... she could disappear forever.

“Enough to buy your retirement, Lena,” Zheng continued. “Enough to buy Paul a dozen startups and Erica her own research institute. But only if you deliver the key. If you fail to convince them, or if they start looking too closely at the ‘donor’ before we hit the Sequoia woods... well, I’ve already spent too much on the logistics to accept a loss.”

The line went dead.

Lena stared at the dark water of the river. She had just been given the coordinates for a fortune, but it felt like a death warrant. She was being told to ask Erica for a list that was, in reality, a shopping list for a kidnapping.

She pulled up Paul’s email on her phone—the one where he wasaskingfor more work.

“Be careful what you wish for, Paul,” she murmured to the wind.

She began to type her reply, her fingers trembling slightly. She didn’t mention the body. She didn’t mention the stolen tech. She just mentioned the one thing she knew would blind them both:The opportunity of a lifetime.
