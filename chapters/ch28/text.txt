Chapter 28 - Following the Breadcrumbs

The FBI's regional field office in San Francisco is a stark, fluorescent contrast to the candlelit stone of the Napa villa. Inspector Pinto stands at a window overlooking the Bay Bridge, the orange glow of the city lights reflecting in his tired eyes. Behind him, Special Agent Vance of the FBI's Cyber and Financial Crimes Division scrolls through a secured data stream.

"You're sure about the link?" Vance asks, tapping a pen against his tablet.

"I've followed the breadcrumbs from Macau to Zurich," Pinto says, turning around. "Lena Vance isn't just a high-end concierge. She is the preferred operative for a ghost we've been calling 'Mr. Z.' We don't have a name, and we don't have a face, but we have his signature."

Pinto slides a physical file across the desk—a rare paper trail in a digital age.

"Mr. Z manages the most sophisticated laundering architecture I've seen in twenty years," Pinto continues. "He's the bridge. He takes black cash from the Mexican cartels—fentanyl moving through the Port of Oakland—and layers it into gray capital fleeing the Chinese mainland. He's cleaning drug money by blending it with the flight of the Chinese billionaire class. Lena handles the logistics for his most sensitive investments. She moves the people."

Agent Vance leans back, studying Lena's surveillance photo. "If she's the key, why are we sitting on our hands while they drink vintage Merlot in St. Helena? We have enough for a warrant."

"We move too early, we lose the chance to turn her," Pinto says, his voice sharpening. "We just got a hit from the credit card processing center. Lena used one of her business accounts to book two suites at the Fairmont for tomorrow night."

"We flip her at the point of maximum vulnerability," Vance says, the cold logic taking hold. "Isolated from her team. Facing a life sentence before the actual heist even begins. We show her that her only way out of a federal supermax is to give us the keys to Mr. Z's kingdom."

"Exactly," Pinto says, looking back at the window. "The scientist and the hacker are just collateral. They think they're part of a grand future. They have no idea they're currently being used as the human shield for the world's largest washing machine."

Vance's phone chirps. "They've finished dinner. The heat signatures are moving toward the master suite."

"Good," Pinto says, picking up his jacket. "Let them enjoy the wine. It's the last taste of freedom any of them are going to have for a long time."
