Chapter 12 - The Celebration
========================================

The shift from the sterile lab to the opulence of the Bürgenstock Resort was dizzying. Perched on a cliff high above Lake Lucerne, the spa was a temple of glass, stone, and steam. Outside, the lake was a sheet of hammered silver; inside, it was all scented oils and soft lighting.

Lena had booked the Royal Suite. It was a strategic move—Zheng’s orders were to “entangle” them, and nothing blurred boundaries like luxury. But there was a catch. Zheng had refused to pay them from his primary accounts.

“The trail must be broken, Lena,”he had told her.“You are the buffer. Pay them from your own wallet. It makes it look like a private business deal between friends.”

The Spa – 05:14 PM

Paul had already vanished into the steam room, exhausted and seeking a “digital detox.” Lena and Erica, wrapped in thick white robes, moved toward the private massage suites.

The room smelled of eucalyptus and lavender. As they lay on side-by-side tables, the tension in Lena’s shoulders began to dissolve under the therapist’s hands. But her mind was still at work.

“Paul looks better,” Lena said, her voice muffled by the face cradle. “The sleep did him good.”

“He’s still buzzing from the recovery,” Erica replied, her voice sounding dreamy. “He doesn’t know about the payment yet. I wanted it to be a surprise. Tomorrow is his birthday, and I still don’t have a gift that tops ‘solving an impossible polynomial.’”

Lena let out a soft, playful laugh. “Well, you’re a neuroscientist. Can’t you just stimulate his pleasure centers directly?”

“I wish. It would save me a fortune in wrapping paper.”

“Honestly?” Lena teased, her heart fluttering as she pushed the boundary. “Every man’s secret dream is simpler than that. He’d probably just want a threesome.”

She meant it as a joke—a way to test the “funk” between them. She expected a laugh, or a polite deflection.

Instead, the room went quiet for a beat.

“You might be surprised, Lena,” Erica said softly. There was no judgment in her voice, only a calm, modern frankness. “Paul and I... we’ve always been very open. We don’t believe in owning each other’s desires. It’s about trust, not exclusivity.”

Lena’s breath hitched. The air in the room suddenly felt much hotter. The joke had turned into an invitation—or at least a wide-open door. She looked over at Erica, whose eyes were closed, a small, knowing smile playing on her lips. Lena felt the 0.454 BTC in her pocket burning a hole through her professional resolve.

The Recovery – 06:30 PM

After the massage, Lena retreated to the marble bathroom to change for dinner. She pulled her phone from her robe pocket.

A notification was waiting.

[04:12 PM] Transaction Confirmed:+1.362 BTC received.

Zheng had moved the funds. The full amount—Paul’s ten percent and Lena’s finders fee—was now sitting in her wallet. She looked at the transaction ID. It was a clean, direct hit from a Hong Kong cluster.

She felt a chill. She was now the “distributor.” If the police ever tracked these coins, the trail ended at her front door. She was no longer just an agent; she was the paymaster for a criminal enterprise.

She tapped out a command, splitting the 1.362 BTC. She sent 0.908 to Paul’s address.

“Happy birthday, Paul,” she whispered to the empty room.

She caught her reflection in the mirror. She looked beautiful, flushed from the heat and the proximity to Erica. But beneath the glow, she saw the truth: she was paying them with stolen money to lead them into a grave.

She smoothed her dress, checked her makeup, and walked out to join the couple for a dinner that cost more than most people made in a month. The “funk” was no longer a spark; it was a forest fire, and Lena was the one holding the match.
