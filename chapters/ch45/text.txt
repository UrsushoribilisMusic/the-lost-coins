Chapter 45: The Depths of the Mill

The ringing in the air was slowly being replaced by the rhythmic shouts of tactical teams and the distant, frantic chirps of police radios. Pinto stepped over a shattered server rack, his eyes still scanning the carnage.

"Lena!" he barked, his voice cutting through the haze of settling dust. "Where are the scientists? Where are Voss and the others?"

Lena scrambled up from the floor, wiping a streak of grease from her forehead. Her cheek was already beginning to swell where a piece of flying debris had grazed her. "The basement. There's an old grain cellar beneath the main floor. Gonzales's men took them down there last night. They've been there for hours."

"Team One, on me!" Pinto signaled to his tactical breach officers. "Lead the way, Lena."

They moved through the ruined annex, past the rows of dead monitors, and down a narrow, lightless stairwell that smelled of damp earth and rot. At the bottom, a heavy iron door stood barred from the outside with a rusted steel bolt. Pinto didn't waste time. He nodded to one of his agents, who stepped forward and set a small, precision thermal charge on the locking mechanism.

BOOM.

The door buckled and groaned, falling inward with a heavy thud.

Inside the cramped, lightless cellar, the beam of Pinto's tactical light cut through the gloom. Paul and Chen were huddled together on the dirt floor, looking like ghosts blinking in the sudden sun. Paul was clutching his ribs, his face pale and drawn, while Chen sat protectively beside him.

"FBI! Clear!" The agent shouted.

Erica was the first to her feet. She didn't look relieved. She looked possessed. She marched straight toward Lena, her eyes burning with a cold, terrifying clarity.

"Did you know?" Erica's voice was a low, dangerous hiss. "Did you know they were going to steal a body? Did you know this was a heist from the beginning?"

Lena opened her mouth to speak, but Erica didn't wait for the answer.

"You sat there and watched them beat Paul!" Erica screamed, her voice finally cracking. "You let them throw us in this hole like animals while you played both sides! You traitor! You absolute scum!"

SLAP.

The sound of the impact echoed off the stone walls. Lena's head snapped to the side, her cheek instantly blooming into a violent crimson. Erica went to swing again, but Pinto intervened, stepping firmly between the two women and catching Erica's wrist in mid-air.

"That's enough, Doctor! Go easy!" Pinto commanded, his voice booming in the small space.

"Get her away from me!" Erica spat, tears finally spilling over as she looked back at Paul on the floor. "She's one of them!"

"No, she isn't," Pinto said, his grip firm but professional. "If it wasn't for Lena cooperating with us, we wouldn't have known you were even down here. If it wasn't for her, you'd probably be dead or buried in this cellar for good. She's the reason you're breathing."

Erica froze, her chest heaving, her eyes darting from Pinto to the silent, bruised Lena. Lena didn't defend herself; she just stood there, her hand pressed against her burning face, staring at the floor.

---

High above them, on the north ridge of the Sequoia forest, the hunt was reaching its inevitable conclusion.

Zheng was running, his expensive wool coat snagged on the brambles, his breath coming in ragged, desperate gasps. He had abandoned his car in the clearing, realizing too late that the forest was his only hope—and his greatest trap. His two remaining guards were ten paces ahead, scanning the dense treeline. They never saw the shadows moving in the ferns.

"POLICE! DON'T MOVE!"

Zheng turned to run, but the air was suddenly filled with the sharp snap-hiss of compressed air.

Two yellow probes arched through the morning light, burying themselves in Zheng's back. Fifty thousand volts surged through his nervous system. His muscles locked, his eyes rolled back, and the man who wanted to own the world's most powerful secret collapsed into the pine needles.

Seconds later, his guards were also on the ground, twitching under the relentless pulse of the FBI's tasers.

"Target Z is secure," a voice crackled over Pinto's radio back in the mill. "We have the principal and his detail. Tased and grounded. Bringing them in now."

Pinto looked at Lena, then at the bruised scientists being led out of the dark. The "Big Fire" was over, but the secrets of the Finnley wallet were only just beginning to surface.
