Chapter 47: The New Growth

The chaos of the mill was fading into the rhythmic, blue-and-red pulse of emergency lights reflecting against the giant Sequoias. The heavy smell of gunpowder was being swept away by the cool mountain breeze. FBI agents, moving with a much gentler touch now that the "Big Fire" was out, guided the survivors toward a waiting ambulance at the edge of the clearing.

Paul leaned heavily on Erica, his face tight with every step, his hand pressed firmly against his side. As they reached the back of the rig, a paramedic assisted him onto the gurney while another turned to Erica with a clipboard in hand.

"I need to run through a quick screen with you," the paramedic said, looking at Erica. "Any pre-existing medical conditions we should know about? High blood pressure? Heart issues? Are you on any daily medications?"

Erica took a breath, her eyes flicking to Paul, who was watching her with exhausted concern. "No," she said, her voice steadying. "But I'm pregnant. About five weeks."

The air in the ambulance seemed to change instantly. Paul sat bolt upright on the gurney, his pain momentarily forgotten as his jaw dropped. "You're... what? Erica?"

Erica reached out, taking his hand, a small, triumphant smile breaking through the grime on her face. "I found out right before the transport. I was waiting for the right moment to tell you, but 'trapped in a dungeon' didn't seem like the best setting."

A look of pure, unadulterated joy broke across Paul's face, an expression so bright it seemed to push back the shadows of the forest. He pulled her hand to his lips, laughing breathlessly despite the ache in his chest. "A baby? Erica, we're... we're going to be parents?"

Chen, who was standing by the open ambulance doors waiting for his own turn with the medic, let out a delighted cackle. He leaned in, his bruised jaw not stopping him from grinning ear to ear.

"Well, look at that," Chen joked, wagging a finger at them. "I knew it. The world definitely needs more Pandas. Just make sure this one learns some actual Kung Fu before he tries to take on the Mafia, okay? We don't need a repeat of your 'lunge and fold' technique."

The first paramedic smiled, moving his hands expertly over Paul's ribcage, applying light pressure to the bruised area. Paul winced, but the medic shook his head reassuringly.

"Take it easy, Dad," the paramedic said with a wink. "Good news for you—nothing is broken. You've got a major blow and some nasty soft-tissue bruising, but the ribs are intact. You'll be sore as hell for a few days, but you'll be fit enough to hold a newborn by the time it arrives."

Paul sank back onto the gurney, his hand still locked in Erica's. He looked out at the mill, where Pinto was overseeing the final processing of the scene. The "Whale" wallet, the billions in Bitcoin, and the greed of Zheng felt like a distant nightmare.

"We're going home," Paul whispered, looking at Erica. "Really home."

"Really home," she agreed, leaning down to kiss his forehead.

Above them, the ancient Sequoias stood silent, indifferent to the rise and fall of fortunes, watching as the survivors of the Big Fire began to plan for a future that had nothing to do with the dead.
