Chapter 27 - The Golden Hour

The sun dips below the Mayacamas Mountains, casting a long amber glow over the private estate in St. Helena. The villa is a fortress of limestone and lavender, overlooking a valley that looks more like a painting than a place of business.

Paul is in the kitchen, engrossed in a tasting led by the estate's private sommelier. The mellow hum of a $400 bottle of Cabernet has finally silenced the frantic tally of debts in his head. For the first time in years, he feels like the winner he always told Erica he would be.

Lena and Erica are on the terrace. The air is warm, thick with wild rosemary and cooling earth. Lena leans against the stone balustrade, watching Erica look out over the vines. The professional distance between them is fraying.

"You're still looking for the catch," Lena says softly.

Erica turns, a half-finished glass of wine in her hand. "I'm looking for the truth, Lena. The papers you gave me... they were perfect. Almost too perfect. And now, this? A villa? A two-day delay that feels like a gift?"

Lena steps closer. "Sometimes a gift is just a gift, Erica. My client wants you focused. He wants you happy."

Erica sets her glass down. Her gaze turns piercing, her posture shifting with sudden, predatory grace. "You're very good at making people feel chosen, Lena." She steps into Lena's personal space, forcing Lena back against the stone railing. "I'm starting to wonder if you're the most dangerous part of this whole equation."

Erica reaches out, her hand firm as she cups Lena's jaw, her thumb brushing Lena's lower lip. The power dynamic shifts in an instant. Erica leans in and kisses her—a deep, assertive kiss that tastes of red wine and defiance. It isn't a question; it's an extraction. Lena's carefully constructed charm shatters. She finds herself responding with desperate, genuine hunger.

"I think I'm starting to see the signal," Erica murmurs against Lena's lips.

"Erica? Lena?"

They pull apart. Paul stands in the French doors, a slow grin spreading across his face. "The chef just sent word. Dinner is served on the west loggia." His gaze moves between them. "I think it's time we moved this inside."

Lena looks at Paul, then back at Erica, and finds herself smiling despite everything.

"Dinner sounds perfect," she says, taking Paul's hand and pulling him into the circle.

As the three of them move back into the warm, candlelit shadows of the villa, the sunset fades to a deep bruised purple. The boundaries of their partnership are about to be completely rewritten.
