Chapter 40: The Swap

The high-definition video link on the monitor flickered, casting a pale glow over the mahogany desk in Gonzales's safe house. Zheng's face was a mask of controlled fury, his voice a thin blade of ice transmitted from a hotel suite in San Francisco.

"I am not interested in biological anomalies, Gonzales," Zheng hissed. "I am interested in results. Dr. Voss says the specimen is a mismatch. This is a catastrophic failure of logistics. I want an onsite meeting. We will resolve this at the lab, in person, tomorrow at 10:00 sharp."

Gonzales tapped a key, ending the call with a sharp snap. He stood up and walked into the adjacent living area, where the two transporters—the men responsible for the utility van's cargo—were waiting. They looked small against the heavy curtains of the safe house, their eyes darting nervously toward their boss.

"Explain it to me," Gonzales growled, stepping into their space. "How does a sixty-year-old billionaire turn into a thirty-year-old woman in the span of a three-hour drive? Did the mountain air do that, or am I being lied to?"

The lead transporter, a man with a jagged scar across his knuckles, wiped sweat from his upper lip despite the air conditioning. "Boss, we did exactly what you said. We had the two canisters at the staging point. We had to move fast—the sensors were already pinging."

"And?" Gonzales barked.

"We had to swap the transponders," the second man mumbled, his voice barely audible. "You told us the decoy had to look like the 'hot' unit to lead the heat toward the coast. We swapped the tags. We moved the transponder from the real patient to the decoy canister so any remote trackers would follow the wrong truck."

Gonzales stared at them, his eyes narrowing as the realization set in. "You swapped the tags... and then you grabbed the wrong box based on the tags you just moved?"

The transporters looked at each other, the silence in the safe house growing heavy. "We were under the clock, Boss. We grabbed the one with the 'Primary' signal. We did as instructed."

Gonzales let out a low, dangerous laugh, shaking his head. "You brought me a random sleeper from the Alcor overflow because you couldn't keep track of your own shell game. You idiots brought a ghost into my lab."

He checked the gold watch on his wrist. It was late afternoon, the sun beginning to dip behind the peaks. "Get your gear ready. We move out tomorrow morning. I expect both of you to be in the car with me. We're going to be at that mill by 09:45 to prep the site before Zheng arrives at ten."

Gonzales leaned in close, his voice dropping to a lethal whisper. "If Zheng finds out my own people botched the handoff because they got confused by their own decoys, he's going to start looking for replacements. And I don't like being replaced. Make sure your weapons are clean. Tomorrow, we fix this—one way or another."
