Chapter 44: The Big Fire

The silence of the Sequoia morning didn't break; it shattered.

Sheriff Miller was three steps from the porch when the mill's heavy timber doors were kicked outward. But it wasn't the Sheriff's warrant that opened them. It was the synchronized thunder of FBI breaching charges.

"FEDERAL AGENTS! FREEZE! POLICE!" Smith's voice roared over the ringing in everyone's ears as a swarm of dark-clad tactical units surged from the treeline.

Inside, the air turned into a soup of cordite and splintered wood. One of Gonzales's young hitters, his nervous system already red-lined from the tension with Zheng's detail, didn't wait for a command. He saw the badges, saw the rifles, and his finger twitched.

Pop-pop-pop.

That was the spark. The mill exploded into a three-front war.

"Down! Get down!" Lena screamed, throwing herself flat against the cold concrete floor as a hail of lead shredded the server racks above her. Coolant hissed from severed lines, mingling with the smoke.

Gonzales's Mafia crew opened up with chaotic, desperate fury, while Zheng's Chinese security detail moved with terrifying precision, forming a wall of lead to cover their principal's retreat. The FBI returned fire with surgical efficiency, their suppressed rifles spitting rhythmic bursts of death.

Outside, Sheriff Miller and his deputy dove behind the door of the cruiser, their handguns drawn but completely outclassed by the automatic fire raking the clearing. "Cease fire! We have locals in the line!" Smith screamed into his headset, but the chaos was already self-sustaining.

Minutes felt like hours. Then, as quickly as it had begun, the heavy thrum of the rifles died down to the sporadic crack of single shots and the groans of the wounded.

"Front clear!" a tactical agent shouted, moving through the ruined entrance. "Left flank secure!" "Rear clear! Multiple targets down!"

Smith stepped into the main hall, his rifle still leveled. The smoke cleared to reveal a graveyard of high-end machinery and broken men. Most of the Mafia soldiers were down; the Chinese detail had been thinned to a few bleeding survivors.

Sheriff Miller and the Alcor Security Chief stepped through the ruined doorway, their faces pale, guns trembling in their hands as they faced a dozen FBI lasers pointed at their chests.

"Mariposa County Sheriff!" Miller yelled back, his voice shaking with a mix of rage and shock. "We have a recovery warrant! Who the hell are you people?"

"FBI, Sheriff. Stand down," Smith snapped, his eyes scanning the room. He spotted a slumped figure by the silver canister. Gonzales was alive, clutching a crimson-soaked shoulder, his face twisted in pain. "Secure Gonzales! Get a medic on him!"

Smith's eyes darted to the clearing outside. Several sets of boot prints led straight from the rear loading bay into the dense, dark forest of the Sequoia ridge.

"Zheng," Smith hissed, his jaw tightening. "He's in the woods. He left his own people to die."

Lena looked up from the floor, her ears ringing, her eyes landing on the "Finnley" canister. It sat there, riddled with bullet holes, the silent legend caught in the middle of a war he never asked for.
