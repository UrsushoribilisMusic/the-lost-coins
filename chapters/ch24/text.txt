Chapter 24 - The Flight
========================================

The air in Zurich Airport's Terminal E is filtered to a sterile, expensive perfection. Paul walks with a bounce in his step that hasn't been there in years, the heavy weight of his laptop bag feeling like a trophy rather than a burden.

"Business class," Paul whispers as they bypass the snaking line for Economy. "I've spent half my life squeezed into 32B next to someone's crying toddler. I didn't even know this carpet was an option."

Lena leads the way, her movements fluid and practiced. She wears a cream-colored silk suit that looks like it cost more than Paul's first server cluster. "Get used to it, Paul," she says, flashing a high-tier status card at the lounge attendant. "When you're working at this level, the logistics should be the least of your concerns."

Erica walks beside her, quieter, her eyes scanning the sleek architecture of the Swiss Air lounge. She's watching Lena—the way she handles the staff, the way she moves through the world as if she owns the air she breathes. The "I'll think about it" from the night before has clearly turned into a "Yes," but the skepticism hasn't entirely vanished; it has just been repackaged as curiosity.

"Champagne?" Lena asks, plucking two glasses from a passing tray as they wait for the boarding call. She hands one to Erica, her fingers intentionally lingering against Erica's skin.

"To new horizons," Erica says, her voice steady. She meets Lena's gaze over the rim of the glass. "And to full transparency."

Lena doesn't blink. "Always."

On the plane—the massive Boeing 777—the luxury reaches a fever pitch. Paul is practically giddy, exploring the seat controls like a child with a new toy. He reclines his pod, tests the noise-canceling headphones, and marvels at the multi-course menu.

"I could stay in this seat forever," Paul laughs, leaning over the partition. "Erica, look at this. They have actual silverware."

Erica smiles at him, but her attention is pulled back to Lena, who is seated across the aisle. The flight is a twelve-hour vacuum, a suspended reality at 35,000 feet. As the cabin lights dim for the long haul over the Atlantic, the game of cat and mouse tightens.

Lena watches Erica through the shadows. She sees the way Erica's brow furrows as she reads a digital paper on her tablet, the way she bites her lip when she's thinking. The attraction is no longer just a "charm" Lena is using; it's a gravity well.

Erica looks up, catching Lena's stare. She doesn't look away. Instead, she unbuckles her seatbelt and moves to the edge of Lena's pod, leaning against the armrest.

"You're watching me again," Erica whispers, the hum of the engines providing a shroud of white noise that isolates them from the rest of the cabin.

"It's a fascinating subject," Lena replies, her voice a low purr.

"The science, or me?"

"Both." Lena reaches up, her hand hovering near Erica's face before she lets it drop. "You're the only person I've met in a long time who isn't distracted by the noise. You're looking for the signal."

Erica leans closer, the scent of Lena's perfume—something dark and floral—filling her senses. "And what is your signal, Lena? Behind the folders and the private donors? Who are you really working for?"

The question is a blade, but Erica's eyes aren't cold. They are searching for a reason to trust the woman who is leading her into the dark.

"I'm working for the future," Lena says, the lie tasting like copper in her mouth. "Your future. Ours."

For a moment, the flight feels like a dream—a high-altitude romance fueled by whiskey and the promise of a billion-dollar ghost. Lena has no idea that ten rows back, a man in a plain charcoal sweater is taking a high-resolution photo of the back of her head. She has no idea that the "Feds" in California are already receiving the manifest.

She just feels the heat of Erica's proximity and the terrifying rush of a plan that is finally, irrevocably in motion.
