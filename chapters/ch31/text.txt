Chapter 31 - The Last Mile

The morning after the villa is soft and blue. Lena drives Paul and Erica south through the Bay Area in companionable silence, the kind that only exists between people who have crossed a line together and have not yet decided what it means.

Paul dozes in the passenger seat, his head resting against the window. Erica sits in the back, scrolling absently through her phone, occasionally glancing at the back of Lena's head with an expression that is still trying to resolve itself.

Lena keeps her eyes on the road. She is thinking about the ultrasound of her sister's baby. She is thinking about Erica's kiss on the terrace. She is thinking about everything except the fact that in forty minutes, she will be walking into Room 412 of the Fairmont Hotel—alone—where everything she has built over the last decade will begin to unravel.

She pulls into the Caltrain station lot, the engine idling. Paul gets out first, stretches, and pulls out their bags with cheerful efficiency. Erica lingers a moment at the car door, her eyes meeting Lena's in the rearview mirror.

"Thank you for Napa," Erica says simply. No calculation. No strategy. Just the truth of someone who hasn't yet learned to be afraid.

"Get some rest," Lena says. "We'll be heading to the site in a few days."

She watches them disappear into the station. Then she pulls back out onto the road.

Two blocks later, she sees it: a silver sedan. Three cars back. Steady. Patient.

She makes three turns. The sedan makes three turns.

Lena does not panic. She keeps her speed even, her hands loose on the wheel. She has been in this situation before—the cold, clarifying geometry of being followed. But this time, she knows she does not have a safe direction to run.

The Fairmont is straight ahead. The clock is already running.
