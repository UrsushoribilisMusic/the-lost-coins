Lena slides a thick cream folder across the bar table
Inside: IRB approvals, donor consent, federal transport manifests

All of it perfect. All of it fake.
Made in Panama by people who specialize in this.

"Why us?" Erica asks. "Why not Stanford or MIT?"
Lena leans in: "I asked around. Your name came up three times."

"You treat the brain like a story instead of a circuit board."
The compliment hits its mark. And Lena means every word of it.

She reaches across the table. Her fingers graze Erica's wrist.
She feels the pulse there. Quick and thrumming.

Erica doesn't pull away. She turns her hand over.
"I want to believe you," she whispers.

"Then come to California," Lena says. Her heart aching.
"Trust me."
