Chapter 46: The Bait and the Switch

The walk from the cellar back to the main lab felt like moving through a graveyard of high-tech ambitions. The air was thick with the smell of ozone, burnt plastic, and the heavy, metallic tang of the shootout. Pinto led the way, while Erica helped a limping Paul, with Chen trailing behind, still rubbing his bruised jaw.

When they entered the main floor, they found the Alcor Security Chief standing by the shattered remains of the silver canister. He wasn't distraught. He was leaning against a worktable with a lopsided, almost predatory grin on his face. Standing next to him, with his hands zip-tied behind his back and a bleeding lip, was the lead transporter—the "switcher" with the scarred knuckles.

Paul stopped, his eyes landing on the canister, now riddled with bullet holes and leaking a slow, hissing stream of nitrogen. Despite the pain in his ribs, a look of profound sadness crossed his face.

"A legend didn't deserve this," Paul said, his voice thick with a mix of exhaustion and reverence. "Finnley... the man who started it all... ending up as collateral damage in a Mafia shootout. It's a tragedy."

The Alcor Chief let out a short, sharp bark of a laugh. He looked at Paul, then at Erica.

"Relax, kid," the Chief said, gesturing to the zip-tied man. "This was never Finnley. We knew these bottom-feeders were planning a swap the moment they started asking about specific transport schedules. So, we let them think they succeeded."

"They thought they were being smart. They had the lady's body—the one from the decoy van—ready to go. But when they made the move in the staging area, the Alcor team had already moved the real Finnley. They left the decoy they thought they were planting right where they'd find it."

The zip-tied switcher looked at the floor, mumbling through his swollen lip. "That's not a legend in there. That's 'Sister Maria.' She was one of Gonzales's bookkeepers who got caught skimming from the laundry last year. Gonzales had her 'stored' for a rainy day. We ended up stealing our own trash."

The room went silent. Paul let out a long, shuddering breath, a massive weight visibly lifting from his shoulders. Erica leaned her head against his arm, a hysterical, relieved laugh bubbling up in her throat. Zheng hadn't just lost the "Whale"—he had started a small war to recover a low-level Mafia casualty.

Chen stepped forward, looking at the server racks that had been shredded by the crossfire. He poked a finger through a perfectly circular hole in a high-end GPU housing, looking at Paul.

"Well," Chen said, his voice bright with its usual dry humor. "On the bright side, Paul, remember how I kept telling you these racks needed better ventilation? I think we finally achieved optimal airflow."

He looked at the wreckage of the expensive hardware and shrugged. "The servers are basically Swiss cheese now. It's very 'modern art.' I call it: The High-Speed Processing of Lead."

Pinto couldn't hide a small smirk. He looked at the scientists—haggard, bruised, but finally free of the Finnley curse. "Alright, 'Kung Fu Panda.' Let's get you to a medic before Chen starts trying to sell the scrap metal to the Sheriff."
