Chapter 21 - The Paper Architect
========================================

The wind off Lake Zug was biting, but Lena barely felt it. She stood on the balcony of her apartment, the encrypted link to Macau active. The silence on the other end after she delivered Paul's message was longer than usual.

"Paperwork," Zheng finally said, the word sounding like an insult. "She wants a trail for a ghost."

"She's a high-level academic, Zheng," Lena countered, pacing the small space. "She doesn't just 'do' jobs. She performs studies. If she doesn't see a donor consent form and a CITES-equivalent transport permit for biological tissue, she won't board that plane. She's afraid of being a 'grave robber.' Her words, not mine."

Zheng's voice dropped into that low, dangerous register. "Then we give her what she needs to sleep at night. I have a team in Panama that specializes in medical foundations. They will produce the 'Ethical Review Board' approvals, the donor's last will and testament, and a federal transport manifest. It will look more official than the HSLU's own archives."

"She'll verify them," Lena warned.

"Let her try. The hospital listed will have a working switchboard. The lawyers will have active bar numbers. We are spending six figures on the logistics of this trip, Lena; a few forged seals are the easiest part of the budget."

There was a brief pause, and Lena could almost hear Zheng's predatory smile.

"But papers only go so far," Zheng continued. "Doubt is a secondary infection. You need to treat the source. You've already established a... rapport with her. Use it. Use your 'charms,' Lena. Make her feel like this isn't just a job, but a shared adventure. If she trusts you, she'll read the papers with her heart instead of her head."

Lena felt a knot tighten in her stomach. The memory of the Bürgenstock suite—the heat, the locked fingers, and the way Erica had looked at her with that sudden, sober clarity—flashed through her mind.

"I'll handle it," Lena said shortly.

"See that you do. The equipment is already being crated in Shenzhen. The 'subject' will be in the Sequoia staging area by the time you land. Don't let a scientist's conscience cost us a thousand coins."

The line clicked shut.

Lena stayed on the balcony for a long time, watching the lights of the city. She felt like a spider caught in its own web. She pulled out her personal phone, her thumb hovering over Erica's contact. She didn't want to manipulate her. She didn't want to be the "charm" in Zheng's arsenal. But she was too deep to turn back.

She typed a short, careful message.

[08:14 PM] Lena: Paul told me about your concerns. I completely understand. I've spoken to the donor's representatives—they're overnighting the full ethical and legal dossier for your review. Can we meet tomorrow for a drink? Just us. I want to make sure you're 100% comfortable before we take this next step.

She hit send and waited, her heart echoing the rhythmic blink of the "delivered" icon.
