Chapter 39: The Signal in the Dark

The Mariposa County Sheriff's office was humming with the low energy of a Tuesday morning, but the high-definition video feed on the main monitor was anything but routine.

"We have a confirmed breach of the secondary containment vault," the man on the screen said. It was the Security Chief for Alcor. "Our internal sensors tracked the primary canister—the 'hot' unit—to an unauthorized staging area. When our field agents arrived, they found a decoy. A shell game, Sheriff. They're playing us."

Sheriff Miller rubbed his eyes, looking at the GPS coordinates on his desk. They pointed directly to the old refurbished mill on the north ridge. "And the real unit? You're telling me it's in my woods? That place has been a shell for a decade."

"Our passive telemetry picked up a low-frequency ping from the cooling system's backup battery," the Alcor Chief replied. "It's at that mill. This was an organized theft by high-end muscle."

Miller leaned back, his chair creaking. He didn't like "outside muscle" in his jurisdiction, but he was a man of the law.

"Look," Miller said firmly. "I can't just storm a private facility on the word of a corporate sensor. If those 'contractors' have a high-priced lawyer, they'll bury me for civil rights violations. I need a judge to sign off on a recovery warrant. The courthouse won't even have a judge on the bench until the morning docket. We're looking at 09:00—nine in the morning—at the earliest."

The Alcor Chief looked frustrated. "Nine in the morning? That's nearly eighteen hours since the breach!"

"Then I suggest you hope their generators are top-of-the-line," Miller snapped. "My deputies will be ready the moment that paper is signed. We'll meet your team at the trailhead at 10:00. We do this by the book, or we don't do it at all. I'm not losing my badge over a frozen head."
