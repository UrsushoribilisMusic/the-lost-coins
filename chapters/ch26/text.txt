Chapter 26 - The Decoy

Scottsdale. Midnight. The Alcor Primary Readiness Wing is silent except for the hum of nitrogen cooling lines.

Jose and Juan, working on behalf of Gonzales, execute a precise operation: replacing a cryogenic storage unit with a decoy containing a deceased woman's body, positioning it to appear as a legitimate patient arrival.

They wheel Unit 803 to a sub-level maintenance area—a camera blind spot—where nitrogen lines are undergoing routine servicing. They load the body into an Apex-series tank, sealing it to pass inspection. The weight differential is negligible. To any automated system, it is indistinguishable from the real thing.

In his monitoring room, security chief Miller notices a brief sensor anomaly: Unit 803 flickers as temporarily missing before reappearing. A junior technician attributes it to the maintenance cycling for new Apex units scheduled for Thursday delivery. Miller dismisses it as a calibration artifact and closes the log.

He has no idea he has just witnessed the first move of what his colleagues will later call the Final Heist. He will only understand the meaning of that blip when the real canister vanishes—and the "new arrival" in the vault turns out to be a ghost.
