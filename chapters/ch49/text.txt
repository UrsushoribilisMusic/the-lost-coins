Chapter 49: The Public Record

The flickering blue light of a wall-mounted television filled the hospital waiting room, casting long shadows over the linoleum floor. Paul, Erica, and Lena sat in a row of plastic chairs, their eyes fixed on the breaking news banner scrolling across the bottom of the screen.

BREAKING: GLOBAL SYNDICATE DISMANTLED IN SEQUOIA RAID

The footage was grainy, likely shot from a news chopper that had arrived just as the FBI was clearing the perimeter. It showed the old mill, now swarming with federal vehicles, looking small and battered against the towering ancient trees.

The screen split. On the left, a handheld camera captured Zheng, his face bruised and his immaculate dark coat covered in forest duff, being shoved into the back of a black armored transport. He looked less like a digital deity and more like a cornered animal. On the right, Gonzales was being loaded into an ambulance under heavy guard, a thick white bandage stark against his shoulder as he snarled at the cameras.

"They look smaller on TV," Chen remarked from a nearby chair, nurse-applied bandages still crisscrossing his face. "Less like monsters, more like... bad casting for a low-budget crime drama."

The broadcast cut to a podium set up near the Mariposa Sheriff's station. Agent Smith stood behind a forest of microphones, his face a mask of bureaucratic iron. He looked exhausted, but his voice was steady, carrying the weight of a man who had finally closed a career-defining file.

"The individuals taken into custody this morning," Smith announced to the flashing bulbs, "represent the head of a sophisticated transnational network involved in high-level money laundering, narcotics, and the attempted theft of sensitive biological assets. This operation was a success due to the unprecedented cooperation between interpol, federal and local agencies."

Smith paused, adjusting his glasses, and looked directly into the main camera lens.

"Furthermore," Smith continued, "the FBI is officially acknowledging the vital assistance of several private citizens who operated at great personal risk to expose this conspiracy. As such, the standing federal bounty of $250,000 for information leading to the capture of Zheng will be processed and distributed immediately to those individuals."

Paul felt Erica's hand tighten in his. Two hundred and fifty thousand dollars. It wasn't the billions promised by the "Whale" wallet, and it wasn't the blood-soaked payout Gonzales had offered. It was clean. It was enough to start over. It was enough for a nursery—or two.

"Well," Paul whispered, leaning his head back against the wall. "I guess we won't be needing those Bitcoin passwords after all."

Erica looked at Lena, who was staring at the image of the mill on the screen. The "Big Fire" was officially out, but as the news cycle moved on to the next headline, the three of them knew the real work—the quiet, complicated work of building a life from the ashes—was only just beginning.
